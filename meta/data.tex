\newcommand{\paperTitle}{Toward a Duality Theory for Feature and Sample Screening}

\newcommand{\paperAbstract}{
Feature screening and sample screening reduce the cost of convex
optimization by removing, respectively, features or samples before the
problem is solved. Although both rely on convex duality, they have
been developed largely independently, model by model, and the
mathematical relationship between the two paradigms has remained
informal. In this paper, we introduce the class of Fenchel--Rockafellar
(FR) representations, on which both screening and FR duality are
defined as transformations. We then prove that screening is equivariant
under FR duality. Thus, the duality between feature screening and
sample screening is not merely an analogy between two types of
screening rules, but a commutative relation between transformations on
FR representations. The proof uses the structural fact that every
screening operator factors into a translation followed by a restriction,
whose primitive forms are equivariant under FR duality. These results
provide a mathematical formulation of the duality between feature and
sample screening, laying a foundation for a systematic duality theory
of screening in machine learning.

}
