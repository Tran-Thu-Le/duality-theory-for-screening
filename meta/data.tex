\newcommand{\paperTitle}{Toward a Duality Theory for Feature and Sample Screening}

\newcommand{\paperAbstract}{
Safe screening accelerates convex optimization by eliminating provably inactive features or samples before solving the optimization problem. Although feature screening and sample screening have been extensively studied, the mathematical relationship between these two paradigms remains unclear. In this paper, we establish a Fenchel duality between feature screening and sample screening: feature screening for a primal problem is equivalent to sample screening for its Fenchel dual, and conversely. To establish this result, we develop a unified framework based on Fenchel–Rockafellar representations, in which both paradigms admit a common decomposition into translation and restriction. Within this framework, several observations previously known in the literature, such as intrinsic complexity reduction and the dual interpretation of simultaneous screening, emerge naturally as consequences of the underlying structure. Our results place screening within a unified mathematical framework, laying the foundation for a systematic theory of screening in machine learning.

}
